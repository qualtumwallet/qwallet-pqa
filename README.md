👉 Dilithium is based on the hardness of Lattice problems (specifically "Learning With Errors" over modules).

🔥 The Quantum ShieldQuantum computers excel at breaking integers and curves (Shor’s Algorithm).

👉 Lattice problems are a different beast. 

Even for a quantum computer, finding the "shortest vector" in a high-dimensional grid is like finding a specific grain of sand in a desert.

⚙️ How it actually works
Dilithium is High-Dimensional Geometry.The Secret ($s_1, s_2$): You pick very "short" vectors (points close to the center of a grid).
The Public Key ($A, t$): You multiply these short vectors by a huge, random matrix $A$. This "flings" the points out into a massive, complex multidimensional space.The Signature: To sign, you prove you know the "short" path back to the center without actually revealing the path.Verification: The verifier checks if your proof fits within a specific "error margin." If it’s too far off, the signature is fake.
